## The Challenge

**Saving the world one nappy at a time**

Children are being potty trained ever later. In the 1940s, most children were potty trained at around 12 months. Now, they are 3 years old on average. This negatively affects the environment and children's health. At the same time many parents struggle with potty training. ## Why Potty Train at Municipal Level?

### Decrease waste disposal costs

Disposable nappies contribute significantly to municipal waste streams. This is usually around 7% of all household waste, leading to high collection and processing costs.

By promoting earlier potty training, municipalities can reduce the volume of waste, directly lowering expenses associated with landfill use, incineration and/or recycling infrastructure.

### Reduce nappy waste & CO2

Disposable nappies create 300 pounds of waste per child per year. For a city with 10,000 inhabitants this amounts to 30 tons of nappy waste on an annual basis.

Sometimes disposable nappies are recycled, but in the Netherlands this is rarely the case. Only 3% of all nappy waste is recycled in the Netherlands due to limited recycling capacity.

### Ensure equal opportunities & health

Nowadays 2 children in each preschool class are not potty trained. In some cases, this means children are not allowed to go to school. Which affects their ability to develop their cognitive and motoric skills.

Besides that, if children start potty training late (after 3 years) they have a higher chance at health issues such as constipation, bed wetting and bladder infections.
