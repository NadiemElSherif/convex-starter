### Q: Can you start potty training too late?

**A:** Yes, starting too late can have health consequences.

**Important Finding**: Several scientific studies show that there CAN be consequences for your child's health when starting potty training too late.

**When It's Too Late**: When starting at a later age (after 3 years), there is a slightly higher chance that your child will develop:
- Incontinence (bed wetting)
- Constipation
- Refusal to poop on a potty or toilet
- Bladder infections

**Health Implications**:
- Late potty training is linked to ongoing health issues
- Early potty training is preventative
- 18 months is ideal for avoiding these problems
- Most children trained by 2-2.5 years avoid most health issues
