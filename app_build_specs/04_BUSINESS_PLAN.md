## 1. Executive Summary

Go Potty is a science-backed potty training platform that combines clinical expertise with modern technology to help parents, nurseries, and municipalities transition children out of nappies earlier, more effectively, and with measurable environmental impact.

**The Problem:**
- The average potty training age has shifted from ~12 months (1940s) to ~3 years today, driven largely by the convenience of disposable diapers
- The global disposable diaper market is a $71 billion industry with no incentive to promote earlier training
- Each child generates 300kg of nappy waste per year; nappies comprise ~7% of household waste
- In the Netherlands, only 3% of nappy waste is recycled
- Parents lack structured, evidence-based guidance; most available methods are one-size-fits-all and reward-based
- 2 children per preschool class are typically still not potty trained, creating workload and cost burdens for nurseries

**The Solution:**
A mobile-first platform offering personalized, expert-supported potty training programs through three revenue channels:
1. **B2C (Parents):** Freemium app with premium coaching at GBP 37.45 for 6 months
2. **B2B (Nurseries):** Licensed dashboards, staff training, and per-child programs
3. **B2B (Municipalities):** Population-level programs with waste reduction tracking and reporting

**Traction:**
- 30+ nursery and municipal partner organizations
- 15+ team members including 2 psychologists, 2 nurses, 1 urologist, 1 physiotherapist
- Partnerships with Leiden University, Kids Konnect, Impact City, BRASPA, Social Enterprise Network
- Active in major Dutch nursery chains (Partou, Babilou, Forte, Heemhuys, Rollebol, Mundo, Prokino) and municipalities (Amersfoort, Zwolle, Alkmaar, Breda, Heiloo)

**Financial Targets:**
- Year 1: EUR 850K - 1.5M revenue (B2C launch + B2B pilot expansion)
- Year 3: EUR 5M - 10M revenue (multi-country, scaled B2B)
- Year 5: EUR 15M - 30M revenue (European market leader)
- Break-even: Month 18-24

5. **Academic partnership**: Leiden University research collaboration provides clinical validation
6. **1-on-1 expert coaching**: Human support as long as needed, not just content delivery
7. **100% money-back guarantee**: Signals confidence in methodology and reduces purchase friction
8. **Existing B2B traction**: 30+ partner organizations is a defensible moat -- competitors would need years to replicate

### 2.6 Market Timing

Several converging factors make this the optimal time to scale:

1. **EU Single-Use Plastics Directive expansion**: Active discussion of including disposable nappies in extended producer responsibility schemes, which would raise nappy costs and increase demand for earlier training
2. **Post-COVID childcare crisis**: Staff shortages across Europe mean nurseries urgently need tools that reduce workload. Potty training is one of the most time-intensive care tasks
3. **Dutch government childcare reform**: The Netherlands is moving toward near-free childcare by 2025-2027, which will increase enrollment and strain capacity, further incentivizing efficiency tools
4. **Parenting app market maturation**: Parents are now comfortable paying for digital parenting tools (pregnancy apps, sleep training, feeding guides). Potty training is a clear gap
5. **ESG and sustainability metrics**: Municipalities need measurable environmental wins. Nappy waste reduction is concrete, trackable, and resonates with voters
6. **Rising diaper costs**: Global inflation in raw materials (wood pulp, superabsorbent polymers) has increased nappy prices 15-25% since 2020, making the "save GBP 500/year" value proposition more compelling

## 3. Business Model

### 3.1 B2C Freemium Model (Parents)

#### Free Tier

Available to all users upon app download. Designed to deliver genuine value and establish trust.

| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Child readiness assessment | Evidence-based quiz determining if the child shows signs of readiness |
| Parent readiness assessment | Self-assessment for parent preparedness (emotional, logistical, time) |
| Method selection tool | Recommends the most suitable potty training approach based on assessment results |
| Basic personalized e-guide | Introductory guide with core methodology overview |
| Community tips (limited) | Access to a curated selection of common questions and answers |
| Push notification reminders (limited) | Basic daily reminders |

**Purpose of free tier:** Build a large user base, generate word-of-mouth, collect anonymized data to improve the product, and create a conversion funnel to premium.

#### Premium Tier

**Price:** GBP 37.45 for 6 months (one-time payment, auto-expires, NOT a subscription)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Full personalized e-guide | Comprehensive, tailored guide based on all assessment results |
| Unlimited tips & reminders | Contextual, stage-appropriate tips and smart reminders throughout the training journey |
| 1-on-1 expert coaching | Direct messaging with a certified potty training consultant; available as long as needed within the 6-month window |
| Full FAQ access | Complete searchable knowledge base of 200+ expert-written Q&As |
| Extended content library | Video guides, troubleshooting flows, night training guides, regression handling |
| Progress tracking dashboard | Visual timeline of milestones, setbacks, and patterns |
| 100% money-back guarantee | Full refund if unsatisfied, no questions asked |

**Pricing rationale:**
- GBP 37.45 represents approximately 1 month of nappy spending (GBP 42/month average)
- If training succeeds even 1 month earlier, the parent saves more than the cost of the program
- The 6-month window is generous (most children train within 2-8 weeks) and reduces urgency anxiety
- One-time payment eliminates subscription fatigue and builds trust
- Money-back guarantee removes all risk from the purchase decision

#### B2C Unit Economics

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) | GBP 37.45 | Premium users only |
| Blended ARPU (all users) | GBP 1.87 - 5.62 | At 5-15% conversion rate |
| Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) per premium user | GBP 8-12 | Consultant time (~30 min avg), infrastructure |
| Gross Margin per premium user | GBP 25-29 (~70-78%) | |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | GBP 5-15 | Blended across channels |
| LTV:CAC ratio | 2.5:1 - 6:1 | Strong economics even at conservative end |
| Payback period | Immediate | One-time payment model |
| Refund rate (estimated) | 5-8% | Based on competitor benchmarks and guarantee policy |
| Net revenue per premium user (after refunds) | GBP 34.50 - 35.60 | |

### 6.3 Consultant Scaling

The 1-on-1 expert coaching is a key differentiator but also the primary scaling challenge. The strategy evolves in three phases.

#### Phase 1: Human-Only (Current - Year 1)

- 8 clinical consultants handle all coaching
- Average 30 minutes per premium user across the training journey
- Capacity: ~800 active premium users per consultant per year
- Total capacity: ~6,400 premium users per year

#### Phase 2: AI-Assisted (Year 2)

- AI chatbot handles Tier 1 questions (common FAQs, encouragement, progress tracking)
- Human consultants focus on Tier 2 (personalized advice, regression handling, complex cases)
- AI handles ~60% of interactions, reducing human consultant time to ~12 min/user
- Capacity per consultant increases to ~2,000 users/year
- Total capacity with 12 consultants: ~24,000 premium users/year

#### Phase 3: AI-First with Human Escalation (Year 3+)

- AI handles 80-90% of coaching interactions
- Human consultants are escalation-only for complex cases
- Average human time per user: ~5 minutes
- Capacity per consultant: ~5,000+ users/year
- Unlimited AI scaling with infrastructure costs only
- Human consultants focus on content creation, AI training, B2B workshops

## 8. Risk Analysis

### 8.1 Market Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Description |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Slow consumer adoption** | Medium | High | Parents may not see the value of paying for potty training guidance when free content exists online |
| **Market education needed** | Medium | Medium | Many parents do not realize earlier training is possible or beneficial; requires awareness building |
| **Nappy industry response** | Low | Medium | Large nappy companies could launch competing free content to protect their market |
| **Economic downturn** | Medium | Medium | Recession could reduce willingness to pay for premium parenting apps, though the "save GBP 500/year" message may resonate more |
| **Cultural resistance** | Low | Medium | Some markets may have stronger cultural attachment to later potty training ages |

### 8.2 Execution Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Description |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Consultant scaling bottleneck** | High | High | 1-on-1 coaching is the key differentiator but hardest to scale; if AI development is delayed, consultant capacity constrains growth |
| **Technical debt from rapid scaling** | Medium | Medium | Moving fast across multiple markets and features could create quality issues |
| **Content quality at scale** | Medium | Medium | Maintaining clinical rigor while localizing to 5+ languages and cultures |
| **Key person dependency** | Medium | High | Small team means loss of CEO, CTO, or lead clinician would significantly impact operations |
| **B2B sales cycle length** | High | Medium | Municipal and large nursery chain sales cycles can be 6-12 months, delaying revenue |

### 8.3 Regulatory Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Description |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **GDPR / children's data** | Medium | High | Processing data about children under 16 requires parental consent and enhanced protections under GDPR Art. 8; any breach would be reputationally devastating |
| **UK Age Appropriate Design Code** | Medium | Medium | Additional requirements for services likely to be accessed by children |
| **Medical advice liability** | Medium | High | If coaching crosses from "guidance" into "medical advice," liability exposure increases; must maintain clear boundaries |
| **Varying regulations across markets** | Low | Medium | Each new country has different data protection, consumer rights, and health advice regulations |
| **Changes to app store policies** | Low | Medium | Apple/Google policy changes could affect pricing, data collection, or in-app purchase requirements |

### 8.4 Competition Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Description |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Large parenting app adds potty training** | Medium | High | BabyBuddy, Huckleberry, or What to Expect could add potty training features, leveraging their existing user base |
| **Nappy brand launches competing service** | Low | Medium | Pampers/Huggies have the brand trust and distribution but a fundamental conflict of interest |
| **Well-funded startup enters space** | Low | High | A well-capitalized competitor could replicate the B2C model; B2B relationships and clinical team are harder to replicate |
| **Open-source / free alternatives** | Low | Low | AI-generated potty training guides could commoditize basic content; coaching and B2B are defensible |
| **Local competitors in new markets** | Medium | Medium | Each new country may have local potty training experts or services with existing relationships |

### 8.5 Risk Mitigation Matrix

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy | Owner | Timeline |
